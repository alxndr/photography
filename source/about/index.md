My father taught me how to use his Pentax SLR as a child, and for my 13th birthday my parents gifted me a 35mm SLR to call my own. In high school I learned my way around a monochrome darkroom, and in college I learned how to develop color and began playing with medium-format film and digital cameras.

I tend to shoot with [Nikon](https://www.nikon.com) cameras, but I've also enjoyed using Olympus and Yashica. Lately my mainstays are a (medium-format) [Zenza Bronica](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bronica) S2A with Nikkor-P f2.8 75mm, a (digital) Nikon zFC with Nikkor DX 18–140mm, and an everpresent iPhone (currently a 17 Pro).

> ![my Zenza Bronica camera](/about/20220331-bronica.jpg)
> _here's the Bronica_

My favorite subject is Mother Nature: plants, animals, landscapes, sunsets…

> ![an artichoke flower](flora/artichoke-flower/2023-06-14-artichoke-flower.jpeg)
> _an artichoke flower_

Other subjects of interest:
* ancient structures…
* street / guerilla art…
* musicians performing live…
* emergent textures in man-made structures…
* the juxtaposition of nature's splendors with the foibles of humanity…

> ![scaffolding in Havana, 2017](/about/20170802-scaffolding.jpg)
> _unusual textures can be fun_
